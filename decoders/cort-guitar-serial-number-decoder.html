How to decode a Cort serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Cort guitar serial numbers primarily indicate the production year, with most models using the first two digits to represent the year (e.g., “06…” indicates 2006, “00…” indicates 2000). Located on the back of the headstock or inside the soundhole, these numbers are not used for specific model identification.

Decoding Cort Serial Numbers

  • Year of Production: The first two digits are the most common indicator of the year (e.g., 06XXXX = 2006).
  • Decade Markers: If the serial starts with a 9 or 0, it likely corresponds to 199X/200X. If the number is newer, 15XXXX would likely be 2015, and so on.
  • Factory Codes: Many Cort guitars, particularly older models, may use letters (like “W” for Cort factory).
  • “Handcrafted” Labels: Some older models may only have “Handcrafted in Korea” or similar labels without a strictly formatted, searchable serial number.

Common Locations

  • Electric: Back of the headstock.
  • Acoustic: Inside the soundhole.

Note: Cort does not have a comprehensive, publicly available online database to lookup model information by serial number alone, so for specific model identification, visual identification is recommended.
