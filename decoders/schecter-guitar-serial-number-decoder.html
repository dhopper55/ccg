How to decode a Schecter serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Schecter guitar serial numbers, typically found on the back of the headstock, generally indicate the manufacturing date using the first two or four digits. For post‑2000 Diamond Series, the format often follows: first letter (factory), next two digits (year), and the following two digits (month).

Key Decoding Details

  • Year (Post‑2008): The first two digits (or digits 2–3 if a letter prefix exists) indicate the year (e.g., “W10…” is 2010, “13…” is 2013).
  • Month (Post‑2008): The third and fourth digits usually indicate the month of production.
  • Pre‑2008: Usually starts with the year, followed by the sequence number (e.g., 02xxxxxx for 2002).
  • Factory Letter Codes: Often start with H or W (South Korea), or other letters indicating specific Asian factories.
  • USA Models: Generally start with a letter indicating the decade, followed by a number for the specific year.

If the serial number is difficult to decode, contacting Schecter directly via their website is recommended for accurate information.
