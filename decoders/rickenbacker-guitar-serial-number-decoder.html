How to decode a Rickenbacker serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Rickenbacker guitar serial numbers, typically stamped on the metal jackplate, are decoded based on production era. Post‑1999 models use a 7‑digit code (YYWWPPL: Year, Week, Production Number). From 1987–1998, two letters indicate month/year, while 1961–1986 used a letter for year and another for month.

Modern System (1999–Present)

  • Format: 7 digits (e.g., 2135123)
  • Digits 1–2: Last two digits of the year (e.g., “21” = 2021).
  • Digits 3–4: Week of the year (01–52).
  • Digits 5–7: Production unit number.

1987–1998 System

  • Format: Letter (Month) + Letter (Year) + 3 or 4 digits.
  • Month Letter: A=Jan, B=Feb, C=Mar, D=Apr, E=May, F=Jun, G=Jul, H=Aug, I=Sep, J=Oct, K=Nov, L=Dec.
  • Year Letter: Starts with A=1987, B=1988, etc.

1961–1986 System

  • Format: Letter (Year) + Letter (Month) + 3 or 4 digits.
  • Year Letter: A=1961, B=1962, …, Z=1986 (Note: J can be 1960 or 1970).
  • Month Letter: A=Jan, B=Feb, …, L=Dec.

Key Tips

  • Location: Always on the metal output jack plate.
  • Exceptions: Some reissues (V‑series) or specialized models may follow different, older, or sequential numbering schemes.
