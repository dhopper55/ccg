Epiphone Serial Number Lookup/Decoder FAQs

Where can I find the Epiphone serial number?

Most Epiphone guitars list the serial number on the back of the headstock. Some acoustics place it inside the soundhole on a paper label, and older instruments may use a neck plate or stamped marking. If you cannot find it, check the headstock back and the neck joint first.

What can this Epiphone serial number lookup/decoder tell me?

It typically identifies the production year, factory or plant code, and country of origin. Some serial formats also hint at model lines or production sequence, but that varies by era.

Why won't my Epiphone serial number decode?

Serial formats change over time, and limited runs or custom shop instruments can deviate from standard patterns. Try removing spaces or hyphens, and if it still fails, reach out so we can review it and improve the decoder.

Note: If you try a serial number and the decoder is not able to decode it, please contact us and let us know so we can check the number and fix the decoder. Thank you!

How to decode an Epiphone serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Epiphone serial numbers generally use a FYYMMRRRR format (Factory, Year, Month, Ranking) since 1993, allowing for easy dating.

  • Letters: Represent the factory (e.g., S = Samick, U = Unsung, R = Peerless).
  • First 2 Numbers (post‑1993): Year of manufacture (e.g., 02 = 2002).
  • Next 2 Numbers: Month (e.g., 05 = May).
  • Remaining Numbers: Unit production number.

Common Serial Number Formats & Decoding

  • Modern (1993–2008): Example S02021234 → S (Samick, Korea), 02 (2002), 02 (February), 1234 (Production).
  • Modern (2008–Present): Example 15122300123 → 15 (2015), 12 (December), 23 (Factory/format code), 00123 (Production).
  • Elite/Elitist Models: Typically F YY SSSS (F = Factory, Y = Year, S = Sequential).

Key Factory Letter Codes

  • B: Bohart, Czech Republic
  • DW: DeaWon, China
  • F: Fuji-gen, Japan
  • I: Saein, Korea
  • J: Pallet, China
  • K: Korea Insung
  • MC: Muse, China
  • R: Peerless, Korea
  • S: Samick, Korea
  • SI: Samick, Indonesia
  • T: Terada, Japan
  • U: Unsung, Korea
  • EE: Qingdao, China

Other Scenarios

  • Pre‑1994: Often 7–8 digits without a factory letter, or sometimes missing entirely.
  • Japanese Models: Often use different, sometimes stamped, formats.
  • No Serial: Some very old Epiphones or refinished models may lack a visible number.
