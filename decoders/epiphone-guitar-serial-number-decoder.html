How to decode an Epiphone serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Epiphone serial numbers generally use a FYYMMRRRR format (Factory, Year, Month, Ranking) since 1993, allowing for easy dating.

  • Letters: Represent the factory (e.g., S = Samick, U = Unsung, R = Peerless).
  • First 2 Numbers (post‑1993): Year of manufacture (e.g., 02 = 2002).
  • Next 2 Numbers: Month (e.g., 05 = May).
  • Remaining Numbers: Unit production number.

Common Serial Number Formats & Decoding

  • Modern (1993–2008): Example S02021234 → S (Samick, Korea), 02 (2002), 02 (February), 1234 (Production).
  • Modern (2008–Present): Example 15122300123 → 15 (2015), 12 (December), 23 (Factory/format code), 00123 (Production).
  • Elite/Elitist Models: Typically F YY SSSS (F = Factory, Y = Year, S = Sequential).

Key Factory Letter Codes

  • B: Bohart, Czech Republic
  • DW: DeaWon, China
  • F: Fuji-gen, Japan
  • I: Saein, Korea
  • J: Pallet, China
  • K: Korea Insung
  • MC: Muse, China
  • R: Peerless, Korea
  • S: Samick, Korea
  • SI: Samick, Indonesia
  • T: Terada, Japan
  • U: Unsung, Korea
  • EE: Qingdao, China

Other Scenarios

  • Pre‑1994: Often 7–8 digits without a factory letter, or sometimes missing entirely.
  • Japanese Models: Often use different, sometimes stamped, formats.
  • No Serial: Some very old Epiphones or refinished models may lack a visible number.
