How to decode a Gretsch serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Modern Gretsch guitars (post‑2002/Fender era) use a straightforward 10‑character serial number found on the back of the headstock. The format is FFYYMMXXXX, where “FF” is the factory code (e.g., JT for Japan‑Terada), “YY” is the year, “MM” is the month, and “XXXX” is the production number. For example, JT04021010 means Japan‑Terada, 2004, February, 1010th unit.

Decoding by Era

  • Modern (2002–Present): Letters (1–2) = factory code (e.g., JT = Japan Terada, CY = China Yako, KP = Korea Peerless, JD = Japan Dyna). Digits 1–2 = year (e.g., 04 = 2004, 15 = 2015). Digits 3–4 = month (e.g., 01 = Jan, 12 = Dec). Digits 5–8 = production number (e.g., 0101–9999 for standard models).
  • Baldwin Era (Aug 1966–1972): Uses a 5–6 digit code stamped on the top or back of the headstock. First 1–2 digits = month (1–12). Next digit = last digit of the year (e.g., 6 = 1966, 0 = 1970). Remaining digits = production number. Note: “Made in USA” was added to stamps starting in June 1967.
  • Pre‑1966/Vintage (1950s–1966): Numbers were generally sequential (e.g., 1000–99999), often stamped on the top of the headstock. Rough dating: <1000 (pre‑WWII), 10xx–20xx (1945–47), 260xx–300xx (1958), 630xx–770xx (1964).

Key Factory Codes (Modern)

  • JT: Japan, Terada Factory
  • JD: Japan, Dyna Gakki
  • JF: Japan, Fuji‑Gen Gakki
  • CY: China, Yako
  • KP/KS: Korea, Peerless/Samick
  • CS: U.S. Custom Shop

For vintage guitars, features (bridges, pickups, logos) are often more reliable than the serial number due to inconsistent recording practices.
