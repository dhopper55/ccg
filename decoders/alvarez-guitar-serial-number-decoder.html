How to decode a Alvarez serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Alvarez guitars can be dated by checking the serial number, typically found on the headstock or a label inside the soundhole. Modern Alvarez guitars (post‑2000) often use an 8–9 digit code starting with a letter, while Alvarez‑Yairi models use a 4–5 digit stamp on the neck heel‑block, sometimes employing Japanese “Emperor” dating codes.

Decoding Alvarez Serial Numbers

  • Modern Alvarez (Post‑2000): These generally follow a format where the letter indicates the factory, and the following numbers relate to the production date. A common format is a letter followed by 8 or 9 digits, where the first two digits often indicate the year, or the last two indicate the year, depending on the model.
  • Alvarez‑Yairi (Vintage/Handmade): These usually have a 4 or 5‑digit number stamped on the internal neck heel‑block.
  • Pre‑2000s: These often use the Japanese Emperor dating system. The first two digits indicate the year of the Emperor’s reign (e.g., 51xxxx = 1976, which is the 51st year of the Showa era).
  • Post‑2000: The Yairi factory shifted to a more standard system, often directly reflecting the year.

Steps to Identify Your Guitar

  • Locate the Number: Look for a stamped number on the neck heel‑block inside the soundhole (common on Yairi) or a label on the back of the headstock.
  • Use the Official Tool: The Alvarez Guitars website has a dedicated date finder and serial number checker for models built in 2000 or later.
  • Contact Support: For older Yairi models, the best method is to use the official Alvarez contact form to get accurate, direct information from them.

Disclaimer: Due to various production eras and manufacturing locations (Japan, Korea, China), serial number formats can vary, making the official lookup tool the most reliable method.
