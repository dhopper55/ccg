How to decode a Jackson serial number

A quick manual method when you don't want to use a decoder.

Jackson guitar serial numbers are generally found on the back of the headstock or neck plate, with modern (2013‑present) import models using a 10‑digit code where the first two digits indicate the year (e.g., ICJ15xxxxxxx is 2015). For older models, especially 1990s Japanese (MIJ) guitars, the first two digits often represent the year, while 7‑digit numbers starting with 96–98 often date from 2002–2008.

Decoding by Region and Model

  • Modern Imports (Indonesia/China/Mexico – 2013‑Present): 10‑digit, e.g., ICJ15xxxxxx. First two digits are the year.
  • Japan (MIJ – 1990–1996): 6‑digit numbers. First two digits = year (e.g., 94xxxx is 1994).
  • Japan (MIJ – Late 1996–2000s): 7+ digit numbers often start with 96, 97, or 98. These are often mid‑2000s guitars, not 1990s.
  • India/Taiwan (1990s): 8–9 digits. Often the first two (India) or first one (Taiwan) digits indicate the year.
  • USA/Custom Shop: Serial numbers are typically stamped on the last fret of the fingerboard (neck‑through) or on the neck plate (bolt‑on). These do not usually follow a direct year‑digit code.

Key Identification Tips

  • Neck Pocket Stamp: The only definitive way to date many Japanese Jacksons is by removing the neck and looking for a date stamp in the neck pocket or heel.
  • Import Serial Check: Use the Jackson Serial Number Lookup for newer models, though it may not include models prior to 2002 or certain import lines.
  • Manufacturer Codes (Import): ICJ = Indonesia, CorTek; NHJ = Chushin Gakki (Japan); JV = Japan (Vintage/Early).

Note: Pre‑2002 records are often unavailable, and many 90s MIJ serial numbers were not officially recorded by factory, making them difficult to track.
